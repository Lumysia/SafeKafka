# Final Project Evaluation Rubric

Real-Time Data Analytics for IoT (40% of Course Grade)

## Project Components

| Component | Weight |
| --- | --- |
| Technical Report | 15% |
| Presentation & Demonstration | 10% |
| Code & System Implementation | 15% |
| Total | 40% |

## A. Technical Report (15%)

| Criterion | Excellent (A) | Good (B) | Satisfactory (C) | Poor (D/F) | Weight |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Problem Definition & Motivation | Clearly defines a significant IoT analytics problem with strong motivation and objectives. | Problem is well defined with reasonable motivation. | Problem definition lacks depth or clarity. | Problem poorly defined or missing. | 3 |
| Literature Review & Background | Comprehensive review of relevant literature and technologies. Demonstrates strong understanding of state-of-the-art methods. | Good coverage of related work with minor omissions. | Limited literature review. | Minimal or no review of related work. | 2 |
| System Design & Methodology | Detailed architecture, data flow, analytics pipeline, and design rationale. | Design is mostly complete and justified. | Basic design with limited justification. | Design incomplete or unclear. | 3 |
| Data Analytics & Results | Strong experimental evaluation, performance analysis, and discussion of results. | Adequate evaluation with reasonable analysis. | Limited evaluation or weak analysis. | Insufficient results or analysis. | 4 |
| Technical Writing Quality | Well-organized, professional, error-free writing with proper citations. | Minor issues in organization or writing. | Several writing or formatting issues. | Poorly written or difficult to follow. | 3 |

Subtotal: 15 Marks

## B. Presentation & Demonstration (10%)

| Criterion | Excellent (A) | Good (B) | Satisfactory (C) | Poor (D/F) | Weight |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Technical Content | Clearly explains objectives, methods, and findings with strong technical depth. | Covers most technical aspects adequately. | Limited technical depth. | Significant gaps in content. | 3 |
| Demo Effectiveness | Live demo successfully demonstrates key system capabilities and real-time analytics features. | Demo mostly successful with minor issues. | Partial functionality demonstrated. | Demo absent or largely unsuccessful. | 3 |
| Presentation Organization | Logical flow, effective storytelling, and professional delivery. | Generally organized and understandable. | Some organization issues. | Difficult to follow. | 2 |
| Questions & Discussion | Demonstrates deep understanding and responds confidently to questions. | Answers most questions adequately. | Limited understanding shown. | Unable to answer questions satisfactorily. | 2 |

Subtotal: 10 Marks

## C. Source Code & System Implementation (15%)

| Criterion | Excellent (A) | Good (B) | Satisfactory (C) | Poor (D/F) | Weight |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| System Functionality | Fully functional end-to-end IoT analytics system. | Most features implemented and functional. | Core functionality implemented. | Significant functionality missing. | 4 |
| Real-Time Data Processing | Effective handling of streaming data, event processing, and analytics. | Mostly effective real-time processing. | Limited real-time capabilities. | Real-time processing not demonstrated. | 3 |
| Code Quality & Documentation | Well-structured, modular, documented, and maintainable code. | Generally clean and documented. | Some code organization issues. | Poor quality or undocumented code. | 3 |
| Innovation & Technical Complexity | Demonstrates advanced techniques, novel ideas, or challenging implementation. | Good technical complexity. | Moderate complexity. | Minimal technical effort. | 3 |
| Reproducibility | Clear instructions, dependencies, and deployment procedures provided. | Minor setup issues. | Setup partially documented. | Difficult or impossible to reproduce. | 2 |

Subtotal: 15 Marks

## Graduate-Level Expectations

To achieve an A-range grade, projects should:

- Process real or realistically simulated IoT data streams.
- Incorporate modern analytics techniques (e.g., stream analytics, machine learning, edge analytics, anomaly detection, predictive analytics).
- Demonstrate measurable evaluation metrics (latency, throughput, accuracy, resource utilization, scalability, etc.).
- Include a working prototype or proof-of-concept implementation.
- Clearly discuss limitations, challenges, and future improvements.
